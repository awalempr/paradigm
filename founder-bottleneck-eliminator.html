Month One — 30 Days

Draw the Line Between Your Decisions and Everyone Else's

The reason decisions keep landing on you is not because your team is incapable. It is because no one has ever drawn the line clearly enough for them to act without checking. When decision authority is undefined, everything migrates to the person with the most accountability, which is always the founder. This phase draws the line and puts it in writing.

Why this matters: Every decision that reaches you unnecessarily carries three costs, the time you spent on it, the time your team spent waiting for you, and the precedent it sets that the next decision like it also comes to you. The first 30 days are about reversing that precedent.
30-Day Actions
ActionWhat to doPriority
Write a decision authority documentMap every recurring decision type to a tier in writing. Tier 1 = team acts alone. Tier 2 = manager judgment. Tier 3 = exec sign-off. Tier 4 = founder only. Publish it to the whole team.critical
Identify your top 3 over-escalated decisionsFrom your misallocation reveal, find the Tier 1 and Tier 2 decisions you are still personally handling. Name the correct owner for each. Set a handoff date, not a gradual transition, a clean cutover.critical
Define spending authority by tierWrite a dollar threshold for each tier. Tier 1 acts below that amount without asking. The absence of defined spending authority is the single most common reason operational decisions escalate unnecessarily.high
Implement a decision bounce-back ruleWhen a Tier 1 or Tier 2 decision reaches you, do not answer it. Route it back to the correct tier with a note identifying where it belongs. This is uncomfortable for two weeks. Then the routing changes.high
Distribute the authority documentSend it in writing, not a meeting. A meeting creates awareness. A document creates a reference. When a team member is uncertain, the answer should come from reading something, not from asking you.medium

Month Two — 60 Days

Give Your Team What They Need to Decide Without You

Authority without context does not produce confident decisions. It produces paralysis followed by escalation. For your team to act at the right tier consistently, they need the information, the criteria, and the confidence that comes from knowing what a good decision looks like in your business. This phase builds those three things into the operating structure.

Why this matters: The bottleneck is almost never a capability problem. Your team is more capable than your current structure is allowing them to demonstrate. Without documented criteria and accessible context, the path of least resistance is always to escalate to the founder. This phase removes that path by making it easier to decide correctly than to ask.
60-Day Actions
ActionWhat to doPriority
Build decision criteria docs for each Tier 2 roleFor every Tier 2 decision your managers own, answer: what information is needed, what does a good outcome look like, and when does it escalate. Managers with this stop asking for your judgment and start exercising their own.critical
Create a business context libraryCompany priorities, active constraints, key client relationships, financial guardrails, and strategic direction, documented and accessible to your team without booking a meeting. When they have context on demand, they make aligned decisions.high
Implement a weekly decision logEvery significant Tier 2 and Tier 3 decision gets logged: who made it, what information they used, what was decided, and the early outcome. This gives you visibility without involvement and builds institutional memory.high
Define escalation criteria in writingNot everything uncertain should escalate. Define the specific conditions under which a Tier 2 decision moves to Tier 3, and when a Tier 3 comes to you. Vague criteria means everything uncertain escalates. Specific criteria fixes most of it at the right tier.high
Run a monthly decision auditReview which decisions escalated last month. Identify which should not have. Adjust tier definitions based on what you learn. The architecture improves every review cycle.medium

Month Three — 90 Days

Protect the Decisions That Need You, and Remove Yourself From Everything Else Permanently

There are decisions that genuinely require the founder, equity, capital, brand repositioning, crisis response. These are not bottlenecks, they are appropriate. The problem is when Tier 4 decisions are surrounded by Tier 1 and Tier 2 noise, creating a business where everything feels like it needs the founder because some things legitimately do. This phase separates the two permanently.

Why this matters: The goal is not to remove the founder from the business. It is to remove the founder from the wrong decisions so they are fully available for the right ones. If you are spending time on Tier 1 and Tier 2 decisions, you are not unavailable, you are misallocated. Your highest-leverage work is getting less than it deserves because your calendar is full of decisions that never needed you.
90-Day Actions
ActionWhat to doPriority
Write your formal Tier 4 decision listEvery decision that genuinely requires you as the founder, written down. Be ruthlessly honest. If something is on the list because of habit or comfort rather than genuine necessity, remove it. The goal is a Tier 4 list short enough to protect and accurate enough to trust.critical
Create handoff documents for each misallocated decision you still carryFor each item your misallocation reveal flagged, document the context, criteria, correct owner, and transition date. A written handoff transfer is what makes delegation real, not a meeting, a document the new owner can reference without you.critical
Build an advisory structure for Tier 4 decisionsThe founder should not enter equity changes, capital raises, or brand pivots alone. A quarterly advisory meeting with two or three experienced operators creates the support infrastructure that makes Tier 4 decisions better, faster, and less isolating.high
Block and protect your Tier 4 timeOnce Tier 1 and Tier 2 are at the right level, the freed time belongs to your highest-leverage work. Block it explicitly. If it is not protected, it will be consumed by what used to fill it.high
Schedule quarterly reassessmentRetake this assessment every 90 days. Track how your correct allocation count changes. A decision architecture that is not measured drifts backward. Founders who measure improve. Founders who do not find the bottleneck quietly returns as the team grows.medium
