# Paradigm Consulting: Off-Site Distribution Playbook

## Why this work matters now

The on-site SEO build is complete. Paradigm has 21 indexable pages with full schema, a Person-schema team page, and a proper hub-and-spoke topic cluster across three pillars and four industries. What it doesn't have yet is **external authority**: backlinks from credible sites, podcast mentions, citations in trade publications, and presence in the directories where buyers look.

Google and the major LLMs both treat external mentions as the strongest authority signal a domain can carry. Without this layer, the existing content will rank for low-competition long-tail queries but will struggle to compete for high-intent terms like *"compliance consulting for direct sales"* or *"AI training for service businesses."* The pillar and industry pages are built. This playbook is how they get the authority to actually win.

## The three workstreams

| Workstream | Format | Time per asset | Cadence | Authority impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Podcast appearances** | 30-45 min interview, founder is guest | 1-2 hours pitch + record per ep | 2-3 pitches/week per founder | Highest per-asset: link + brand mention + LLM training data + listener funnel |
| **Guest content** | 800-1500 word article published on a third-party site | 4-8 hours per piece | 1 published per month per founder | High: link from authority domain + brand-narrative control |
| **Directory submissions** | Listing page on a directory site | 30-60 min per directory | Batch 5-10 per week until done | Medium per-link but stacks: 30-50 baseline domain-rating signals |

Each workstream is in its own playbook file:

- **[Podcast outreach](01-podcast-outreach.md)** — sourcing podcasts, pitch templates per founder
- **[Guest content](02-guest-content.md)** — target publications, pitch templates, article angles
- **[Directory submissions](03-directories.md)** — directory list, submission template, tracking

## How to sequence the first 90 days

The compounding move is **podcasts first, then guest content, then directories in parallel**. Podcasts produce fastest signal (one good appearance can generate 1,000+ branded searches in the weeks after) and they create the relationships that warm up guest-post pitches later.

### Days 1-30: Podcast pitching engine

- Send 6-9 pitches per week across the three founders (2-3 each)
- Track: pitches sent, replies, booked, recorded, published
- Goal: 2 booked episodes by day 30
- Side effect: each founder learns to tell the Paradigm story tightly in 30 minutes, which becomes the spine of every other piece of outreach

### Days 31-60: First guest content + first directory batch

- Pitch 4-6 publications using podcast appearances as warm context ("Heard our podcast with X? Here's a piece that builds on that thesis...")
- Submit to 15-20 directories (see [03-directories.md](03-directories.md))
- Continue podcast cadence
- Goal: 1 published guest article + 15 directory listings live by day 60

### Days 61-90: Compounding

- Add: monthly publication of one piece on Paradigm's own site that gets pushed into guest-content pitches
- Audit: which podcasts produced real signal (branded search lift, inbound, links). Double down on hosts who promote
- Goal: 3+ published podcasts, 2+ guest articles, 20+ directory listings, first measurable lift in branded search

## Metrics to track (weekly)

| Metric | Where to read it | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Pitches sent | Spreadsheet (separate column per founder × per workstream) | Activity is happening |
| Reply rate | Same spreadsheet | Pitch quality is right |
| Booked / accepted | Same spreadsheet | Targeting is right |
| Published | Same spreadsheet | Throughput |
| Referring domains | Ahrefs, Semrush, Google Search Console | Compounding authority signal |
| Branded search volume | Google Search Console, Google Trends | Outreach is producing brand demand |
| LLM citation appearances | Manual checks: ask Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Google AI Overviews about industry topics; note when Paradigm is cited | Whether the schema and content is doing its job downstream |

## Who runs what

The work splits cleanly by founder when the topic is theirs. Operationally one person can pitch and coordinate for all three founders.

- **Compliance (Ari) topics**: FTC enforcement, ROSCA, classification, multi-state licensing
- **Culture (Matt) topics**: producer/distributor retention, sales culture, leadership, recruiting
- **Tech (Jay) topics**: AI for service businesses, agentic workflows, automation, dispatch optimization

Pitches go out under the right founder's name. Track records and bios are sourced from [/team](https://paradigmconsulting.io/team), which is the canonical bio page for all three.

## When to stop / when to scale

This playbook is built to run for 6-12 months and produce a compounding authority profile. If the pitch reply rate falls below 5% for more than 4 weeks in a row, pause and audit pitch copy and target list. If it's above 15%, double the volume.

The successful end state is a Paradigm Consulting that, when an operator searches "how do I scale sales reps without misclassifying them" or asks ChatGPT "who builds compliance systems for service businesses," surfaces in the first answer.
