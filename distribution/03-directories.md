# Workstream 03 — Directory Submissions

## What this workstream does

Directory submissions are the lowest per-asset authority impact of the three workstreams — but they stack. A single directory listing might be worth a fraction of a podcast appearance, but 30-50 of them produce a compounding baseline of referring domains that elevate the entire site's domain rating, surface the brand in buyer-side search ("compliance consulting for direct sales firms" → buyer scrolls past Google ads to see who's listed in The Manifest, Clutch, DSN), and feed the LLMs structured business data they use to answer "who does X" queries.

This is the cheapest workstream by time-per-asset (30-60 minutes per directory) and the easiest to delegate. Run it in parallel with podcast outreach and guest content. The goal is 30+ live listings within the first 90 days.

---

## How directories actually move the needle

Three distinct benefits, in declining order of impact:

1. **Authority signal to Google.** Each accepted listing adds a referring domain. Domain rating is logarithmic — first 20 quality referring domains move it more than the next 200. Concentrate on Tier 1.
2. **Buyer-side discovery.** Operators looking for compliance, culture, or AI consulting frequently search through directories rather than open web search. The Manifest, Clutch, DSN's vendor list, and industry-specific listings put Paradigm in the consideration set at the moment of intent.
3. **LLM training data.** Structured business listings on high-trust sites become part of the data ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews use to answer business-recommendation queries. The more consistent and complete the listings, the more reliably Paradigm surfaces.

## Submission workflow

1. **Batch credentials.** Use a dedicated email (`directories@paradigmconsulting.io` or a Gmail filter) for confirmation emails. Many directories require email verification; centralizing this prevents lost confirmations.
2. **Pre-fill the boilerplate.** All canonical data lives in one shared doc. Per-directory edits are minor (category names vary, character limits vary).
3. **Submit in batches of 5-10.** Block 90-120 minutes per batch. Run 1-2 batches per week.
4. **Track every submission immediately.** A submission you don't track is a backlink you can't audit.
5. **Verify within 14 days.** Some directories take 1-2 weeks to approve. After 14 days, check status; resubmit if rejected.
6. **Audit live status quarterly.** Some directories silently delist after 6-12 months. Quarterly link-rot audits keep the asset live.

## Cadence and quotas

- **Week 1-2:** Stand up the boilerplate doc and a shared submission email. Submit the 8 Tier 1 directories. ~6 operator-hours total.
- **Week 3-6:** Submit Tier 2 industry-specific listings. ~8-10 listings depending on which industries are active. ~10 operator-hours total.
- **Week 7-8:** Submit Tier 3 speaker / expert directories — one profile per founder, three founders. ~6 operator-hours total.
- **Week 9-12:** Submit Tier 4 founder / personal brand directories and Tier 5 backfill in batches. ~8 operator-hours total.

**90-day target:** 30+ live listings, 15+ from Tier 1 and 2 (the ones that actually move the needle).

**Total operator time, 90 days:** ~30 hours, batched into ~10 sessions of 2-3 hours each.

---

## Hard rules

- **Consistency across listings.** Same name, same tagline, same description, same logo, same phone. LLMs cross-reference; inconsistency degrades the citation pattern.
- **Do not pay for promoted slots in Tier 1 until organic listings are live.** Promoted directory placements rarely produce the inbound volume to justify the cost at Paradigm's stage.
- **Refuse "directory submission services."** They're spam farms. Each submission is fast enough to do directly.
- **Submit under a real human, not the operator's name.** Most directories show submitter as a contact; use one of the founders (Matt as the default since he's the public-facing founder).
