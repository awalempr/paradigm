# Workstream 01 — Podcast Outreach

## What this workstream does

Podcast appearances are the highest-leverage authority asset Paradigm can produce in the next 90 days. One 45-minute interview puts a founder's voice in front of a pre-qualified audience, generates a backlink from the show's site and shownotes, gives Google and the LLMs new "this person is an authority on X" training data, and creates a relationship with a host that warms up every future ask (guest posts, intros, referrals).

The cost is time: ~90 minutes to pitch, ~60 minutes to record, ~30 minutes post-publication promotion. Per founder, that is roughly four hours per booked appearance — and the asset compounds for years.

This workstream is run by an operator pitching under each founder's name. The founders do not pitch their own shows. They show up, record, and promote.

## Sourcing process

This is the part most operators get wrong. Sourcing podcasts is not a Google search. It is a structured search across three channels, ranked by reply rate.

### 1. Listen Notes (highest reply rate)

- listennotes.com — search by topic, filter by recency (last 60 days of episodes), audience size (1k+ listeners per episode), and "accepts guests."
- For each result, open the host's profile, find their direct email (usually in show notes or `show.com/contact`).
- Save: show name, host name, host email, audience estimate, last episode date, three recent episode topics (proves you actually listened).

### 2. Spotify + Apple category search

- Browse the relevant category (Business, Marketing, Entrepreneurship, Industry-specific).
- Sort by "popularity" or "new and noteworthy" — these are the shows actively booking guests.
- For each candidate, click through to the show's website to find the booking process.

### 3. Cross-reference Apollo and LinkedIn

- Once you have a host name, run them through Apollo to find a verified email if the show's contact page doesn't list one.
- Connect on LinkedIn — do NOT pitch in the connection request. Connect first, pitch via email later. LinkedIn pitches at this stage tank reply rate.

Target: 30 sourced shows per founder before the first pitch goes out. This is one operator-day of sourcing per founder.

## Pitch templates

Every pitch is one operator pitching under one founder's name. Keep it under 150 words, name a specific recent episode, lead with the angle, end with a soft ask.

### Template — Tier 1 pitch

```

Hi [HOST FIRST NAME],

I caught your episode with [RECENT GUEST] on [SPECIFIC TOPIC] last [TIMEFRAME]
— [ONE SENTENCE ON WHAT LANDED, SPECIFIC, NOT GENERIC].

I'm writing on behalf of [FOUNDER FULL NAME], [TITLE] at Paradigm Consulting.
[FOUNDER FIRST NAME] has [ONE-LINE CREDENTIAL — e.g., "structured compliance
frameworks for over $2.5B in sales volume across direct selling and insurance"].

The angle I'd pitch for your audience is:

> [SPECIFIC EPISODE ANGLE — 1-2 sentences, framed as a question your audience
> would want answered]

Happy to send three more angles if this one isn't a fit. [FOUNDER FIRST NAME]'s
bio is at https://paradigmconsulting.io/team#[ANCHOR] if you want to take a look.

Worth a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit?

[OPERATOR NAME]
on behalf of [FOUNDER FIRST NAME] | Paradigm Consulting
```

## Pre-record prep checklist (founder-side)

The day before recording, the founder spends 30 minutes prepping:

- [ ] Listen to the last two episodes of the show. Note host's interview style and recurring questions.
- [ ] Review the three committed talking points. Have one specific story or stat for each.
- [ ] Have the Paradigm one-liner ready: "Paradigm Consulting installs compliance, culture, and technology systems for service businesses scaling between $5M and $50M."
- [ ] Have a written CTA the host can read at the end: "Listeners can find the [risk map / culture systems / tech systems] page at paradigmconsulting.io/[slug]."
- [ ] Test mic, lighting, and internet. Backup hotspot if WFH.
- [ ] Block 30 min after recording for any retakes the host requests.

## Post-publication promotion

The episode is the asset. The promotion is what makes the asset compound.

When an episode goes live:

1. **LinkedIn post under the founder's profile.** Tag the host. Quote one specific moment. Link to the episode. Native upload (don't link out as primary; LinkedIn de-ranks external links — embed the audio or post the quote with the link in comment).
2. **Email to Paradigm list.** One paragraph + episode link.
3. **Add to /team page** — each founder's bio gets a "Recent appearances" list. Update after every published episode.
4. **Add to internal tracker.** Date, show, host, episode URL, branded search lift over 30 days (Google Search Console), inbound mentions (LinkedIn search), referral traffic to paradigmconsulting.io.

## Cadence and quotas

- **Sourcing:** 30 shows per founder, refreshed monthly. Stop sourcing once 30 unpitched targets are in the queue.
- **Pitching:** 2-3 pitches per founder per week. 6-9 total per week.
- **Follow-up:** Day 5 if no reply. Day 14 if no reply to follow-up. Then move to "circle back in 3 months" list.
- **Booking → recording lag:** Target < 21 days. Longer lags lose energy.
- **Recording → publication lag:** Most shows publish 2-6 weeks after recording. Tracked but not in our control.

Reply rate benchmarks:
- **Below 5%:** Pitch quality or list quality is wrong. Pause and audit. Either the angle is generic, the host doesn't book outside guests, or the show is bigger than the pitch is positioned for.
- **5-12%:** Working. Continue.
- **Above 15%:** Either the list is too warm (you're under-pitching) or the angles are exceptionally good. Double volume.

---

## When this is working

- 3-5 booked recordings per month across all three founders (30-50% reply rate on a well-warmed list).
- 2+ published episodes per month after the lag washes out.
- Branded search for "Paradigm Consulting" + founder names rising month-over-month in GSC.
- First inbound DM or email citing a specific episode within 60 days of the first published appearance.
