What's at stake

Most service businesses hit a ceiling at $5M. The ones who break through install infrastructure.

Founder-led businesses scale on energy and instinct until the moment that stops working — usually somewhere between $2M and $10M. What follows is one of four outcomes, and the 3x3 OS is built to surface which one you are walking into before it surfaces you.

Founder Dependency
$0

What the business is worth on the day you stop showing up.

If the operation requires you in the room to make decisions, hold accountability, or keep the team aligned, the company is not an asset — it is a job. Buyers price it that way. So does your nervous system.

Valuation Ceiling
5x → 12–15x

Multiple expansion gap between founder-dependent and system-operated businesses.

Service businesses without documented infrastructure cap out at 4x–5x EBITDA at exit. The same business with culture, compliance, and technology systems installed clears 12x–15x. The gap is operational, not financial.

Compliance Exposure
$7M–$200M

Range of FTC consent orders in recent service-business enforcement actions.

Income-claim language, ROSCA gaps, rep classification, multi-state licensing — each one resolvable in 30 to 60 days if surfaced early, each one a six-figure remediation once a regulator gets there first.

Culture Collapse
60–80%

Turnover rate in service-business roles where culture lives in the founder's head.

When culture is undocumented, every new hire is a guess and every senior departure resets the operating norms. Top-line growth becomes impossible because the team can't hold what the founder built.

Chaos → compounding infrastructure. Founder-dependent → leader-independent. 5x valuation ceiling → 12–15x post-install. The Self-Paced Audit is how founders see which of those transitions they are closest to making — and which pillar is currently blocking the next one.
